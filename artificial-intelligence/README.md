# Artificial Intelligence

Welcome to the Artificial Intelligence track of the **Syed's Open Source Computer Science Curriculum (SOCC)**.

This track is designed to take learners from the fundamental foundations of Artificial Intelligence to building production-grade AI systems and AI agents.

Unlike many AI courses that focus only on using existing tools, this curriculum emphasizes understanding how modern AI systems work, why they work, and how to build them from first principles.

---

# Curriculum Structure

## Part I — Foundations of Artificial Intelligence

Build a strong conceptual and mathematical foundation for modern Artificial Intelligence.

- Chapter 1 — The Big Picture of Modern Artificial Intelligence
- Chapter 2 — Linear Algebra
- Chapter 3 — Calculus
- Chapter 4 — Probability & Statistics
- Chapter 5 — Optimization

---

## Part II — Building AI: From Tensors to Agents

Learn how AI systems are implemented from scratch, progressing from basic mathematical operations to neural networks and modern AI architectures.

---

## Part III — Building Large Language Models

Understand the complete lifecycle of modern LLMs, from tokenization and transformers to training, fine-tuning, alignment, inference, and deployment.

---

## Part IV — Agentic AI Systems

Build autonomous AI agents capable of reasoning, planning, memory management, retrieval, and tool use.

---

## Part V — Production AI Engineering

Deploy, optimize, evaluate, monitor, and scale AI systems for real-world production environments.

---

## Part VI — Frontier AI

Explore current research directions including multimodal AI, reasoning models, world models, robotics, scientific AI, and emerging paradigms.

---

## Part VII — AI Specializations

Advanced topics and domain-specific applications of Artificial Intelligence.

Examples include:

- Computer Vision
- Natural Language Processing
- Reinforcement Learning
- AI for Robotics
- AI for Cybersecurity
- AI for Healthcare
- Knowledge Graphs
- Graph Neural Networks
- Time-Series AI
- Recommendation Systems
- AI Safety
- AI Alignment

---

# Learning Philosophy

> **Understand first. Build second. Optimize third.**

Every topic answers four questions:

- What is it?
- Why does it exist?
- How does it work?
- How do we build it?

The goal is not merely to use AI tools, but to understand and engineer them with confidence.
